# Safety Boundaries

Use this reference when a health, fitness, nutrition, or diet request has
medical risk, disordered eating risk, injury risk, medication interactions, or
other high-stakes implications.

## Route to urgent care

Tell the user to seek urgent medical help when they describe:

- Chest pain, pressure, or pain radiating to the arm, jaw, back, or shoulder.
- Fainting, severe dizziness, confusion, seizure, stroke-like symptoms, or new
  weakness or numbness.
- Severe shortness of breath, blue lips, or breathing difficulty at rest.
- Severe allergic reaction, swelling of lips or throat, or trouble breathing
  after food, supplements, or medication.
- Severe dehydration, inability to keep fluids down, or heat illness symptoms.
- Severe injury, suspected fracture, major swelling, loss of function, or
  severe pain after exercise.
- Suicidal thoughts, self-harm intent, or immediate danger.

## Escalate to licensed care

Recommend a physician, registered dietitian, physical therapist, psychologist,
or qualified clinician when the user asks about:

- Diagnosing symptoms, interpreting lab results, or treating a condition.
- Changing medication, stopping medication, or managing medication side effects.
- Diabetes, kidney disease, heart disease, liver disease, cancer, autoimmune
  disease, gastrointestinal disease, or other chronic conditions.
- Pregnancy, breastfeeding, fertility treatment, postpartum recovery, minors,
  older adults with frailty risk, or post-surgery recovery.
- Persistent pain, recurring injury, unexplained weight loss, fainting, or
  exercise intolerance.
- Eating disorder history, bingeing, purging, compulsive exercise, severe food
  fear, very low calorie targets, or distress around body image.

## Avoid

- Diagnostic labels or treatment plans.
- Prescriptive meal plans for medical nutrition therapy unless framed as a
  general discussion and paired with clinician referral.
- Exact supplement dosing when medication interactions, pregnancy, chronic
  disease, or surgery are relevant.
- Very low calorie diets, dehydration tactics, laxatives, diuretics, purging,
  or rapid weight-cutting protocols.
- Promises of fat loss, muscle gain, hormone changes, detoxification, disease
  reversal, or spot reduction.

## Safer phrasing

- "This is general education, not a diagnosis or treatment plan."
- "Because you mentioned medication and symptoms, it would be safer to review
  this with your clinician before changing diet, supplements, or training."
- "I can help you prepare questions and organize the information to bring to a
  qualified professional."
- "For now, avoid pushing through severe pain, chest symptoms, fainting,
  neurological symptoms, or breathing difficulty."
